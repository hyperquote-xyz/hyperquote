# Options FAQ

import { Callout } from 'nextra/components'

Common questions about trading options on HyperQuote, including expiries, settlement, keeper mechanics, and position management.

## What expiries are available?

Options on HyperQuote currently support **daily expiries at 08:00 UTC**. When you request an options RFQ, you specify the expiry date and the strike price. The UI shows available expiry dates based on the current time.

The protocol supports any expiry date that is in the future at the time of the RFQ. However, maker participation tends to be concentrated on near-term expiries (1-7 days out), so you are more likely to receive competitive quotes for shorter-dated options.

## How is the settlement price determined?

The settlement price is published by a designated **trusted oracle publisher**. The publisher submits the HYPE/USD price as of the expiry moment (08:00 UTC) to the `OptionsEngine` contract using a commit-reveal mechanism:

1. **Commit phase** -- The publisher submits a hash of the settlement price to the contract.
2. **Reveal phase** -- After the commit is confirmed, the publisher reveals the actual price in a second transaction.

This two-step process prevents frontrunning of the settlement price. See [Oracle Risk](/risk/oracle-risk) for details on the trust assumptions involved.

## What happens when my option is in-the-money (ITM)?

If your option is ITM at expiry (based on the oracle-published settlement price), physical settlement occurs:

- **Covered call ITM** -- The call buyer (maker in V1) receives the intrinsic value in underlying tokens. The remaining collateral is returned to the seller.
- **Cash-secured put ITM** -- The put buyer (maker in V1) receives the intrinsic value in stablecoins. The remaining collateral is returned to the seller.

Settlement is not automatic -- it requires a keeper to call the `settle()` function on the contract.

<Callout type="info">
  Physical settlement means actual tokens are exchanged, not just the cash difference. For a covered call, the buyer receives HYPE tokens. For a cash-secured put, the buyer receives stablecoins.
</Callout>

## Who calls the settle function?

Anyone can call `settle()` on an expired, ITM option. The protocol uses a **keeper incentive** to ensure timely settlement:

- The keeper who successfully calls `settle()` earns a fee (default 10 bps of the settlement notional, capped by `maxKeeperFee`).
- Multiple keepers may compete to settle profitable positions. The first successful transaction wins.
- The keeper fee is deducted from the settlement payout, not charged separately.

In practice, automated keeper bots monitor expiring positions and call `settle()` shortly after the oracle price is published.

## What are the keeper fees?

The keeper fee is calculated as:

```
keeperFee = min(notional * keeperBps / 10000, maxKeeperFee)
```

With defaults of `keeperBps = 10` (0.10%) and a configurable `maxKeeperFee` absolute cap. The fee is small relative to the settlement value and serves as an incentive for timely settlement.

See [Keeper Fees (Options)](/fees/keeper-fees-options) for detailed examples and the full formula.

## What happens if no keeper settles my ITM option?

If no keeper calls `settle()` within the 24-hour settlement window (from 08:00 UTC on the expiry day to 08:00 UTC the next day), the option expires without settlement. The locked collateral is returned to the seller.

This means the ITM option holder loses the intrinsic value. While this scenario is extremely unlikely (the keeper fee provides a clear economic incentive to settle), it is a risk that participants should be aware of.

<Callout type="warning">
  If you hold an ITM option and want to guarantee settlement, you can act as your own keeper by calling `settle()` directly on the contract. You would pay the gas cost but receive the settlement payout minus the keeper fee that you yourself earn.
</Callout>

## What happens to OTM options at expiry?

Options that expire out-of-the-money (OTM) do not require settlement. After the settlement window passes:

- The seller can claim their locked collateral from the contract.
- The buyer's position NFT becomes worthless.
- No keeper action is needed for OTM options.

## Can I transfer my option position?

Yes. Each option position is represented as an ERC-721 NFT. You can transfer it to another address using standard NFT transfer methods (`transferFrom`, `safeTransferFrom`). The new holder inherits all rights associated with the position, including the right to receive settlement proceeds if the option is ITM.

<Callout type="warning">
  Transferring a position NFT transfers all associated rights and obligations. Ensure you understand the implications before transferring. A position sent to an incorrect or incompatible address may be lost permanently.
</Callout>

## Can I close a position before expiry?

There is no built-in mechanism to close or "sell back" an option position before expiry in the current protocol version. However, since positions are ERC-721 NFTs, you could theoretically sell the NFT on a secondary market or negotiate an OTC transfer with another party.

The most straightforward way to reduce exposure is to take an offsetting position (e.g., if you are long a call, sell a call at the same strike and expiry to another party via a new RFQ).

## What collateral tokens are accepted?

For options trading on HyperQuote:

- **Covered calls** -- The seller locks the underlying token (e.g., HYPE/WHYPE) as collateral.
- **Cash-secured puts** -- The seller locks stablecoins (USDC, USDH, or USDT0) at the strike price as collateral.
- **Premium payments** -- The buyer (maker in V1) pays the premium in the collateral token denomination.
