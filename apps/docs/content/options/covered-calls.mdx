# Covered Calls

import { Callout } from 'nextra/components'

A covered call is an options strategy where the seller locks underlying tokens and grants the buyer the right to purchase them at a predetermined strike price on the expiry date. The buyer pays a premium upfront for this right.

## How It Works

In HyperQuote, the **taker is always the seller** and the **maker is always the buyer** (V1 constraint). When a covered call is executed:

1. The **seller** (taker) locks their underlying tokens (wHYPE) in the OptionsEngine contract as collateral.
2. The **buyer** (maker) pays a premium in the collateral stablecoin (USDC, USDH, or USDT0) to the seller.
3. An ERC-721 position NFT is minted to the buyer representing their right to buy the underlying at the strike price.

<Callout type="info">
The seller receives the premium immediately at execution time. The locked underlying tokens are held by the contract until expiry.
</Callout>

## Collateral for Covered Calls

For a covered call, the collateral locked is simply the **quantity of underlying tokens** specified in the quote. The seller must have approved and hold at least this amount of wHYPE.

```
collateralLocked = quantity
```

For example, selling a covered call on 10 wHYPE means the seller locks exactly 10 wHYPE in the contract, regardless of the strike price.

## Settlement: In-the-Money (ITM)

A covered call is ITM when the settlement price exceeds the strike price (`S > K`). When settled:

1. The **buyer** delivers stablecoin equal to `strike * quantity` (scaled to collateral decimals) to the contract.
2. The **contract** sends the locked underlying tokens (wHYPE) to the buyer.
3. The **contract** sends the stablecoin payment to the seller, minus the keeper fee.

In effect, the seller sells their wHYPE at the strike price, and the buyer acquires the underlying at a discount to the current market price.

### Example

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Underlying | wHYPE |
| Collateral | USDC |
| Strike | $25.00 |
| Quantity | 100 wHYPE |
| Premium | 50 USDC |
| Settlement Price | $30.00 |

The call is ITM ($30 > $25). At settlement:

- Buyer delivers **2,500 USDC** (25 * 100) to the contract
- Buyer receives **100 wHYPE** from the contract
- Seller receives **2,500 USDC** minus keeper fee
- Seller already collected **50 USDC** premium at execution

The seller's effective sale price is $25.50 per wHYPE (strike + premium per unit). The buyer's effective purchase price is $25.50, compared to a market price of $30.

## Expiry: Out-of-the-Money (OTM)

A covered call is OTM when the settlement price is at or below the strike (`S <= K`). The option expires worthless:

1. The **seller's** locked underlying (wHYPE) is returned in full.
2. The **seller** keeps the premium collected at execution.
3. The position NFT is burned.

### Example

Using the same parameters above but with a settlement price of $20:

- Seller gets back **100 wHYPE**
- Seller keeps **50 USDC** premium
- Buyer's option expires worthless (no rational reason to buy at $25 when market is $20)

## When to Sell Covered Calls

Covered calls are typically attractive when:

- You hold the underlying token and want to generate yield from premiums
- You have a neutral-to-slightly-bullish outlook and are willing to sell at the strike price
- You want to reduce the effective cost basis of your holdings

<Callout type="warning">
If the underlying price rises significantly above the strike, you will have sold your tokens at below market value. Your upside is capped at the strike price plus the premium received.
</Callout>

## On-Chain Mechanics

The OptionsEngine handles covered call creation in `_createPosition`:

```solidity
if (quote.isCall) {
    collateralAmount = quote.quantity;
}
// ...
// Lock collateral from seller (underlying for calls)
if (quote.isCall) {
    IERC20(quote.underlying).safeTransferFrom(
        seller, address(this), collateralAmount
    );
}
```

At settlement, the `_executeSettlement` function handles the physical delivery:

```solidity
if (pos.isCall) {
    // Buyer delivers strike * qty in collateral (stablecoin)
    uint256 grossCollateral = CollateralMath.callSettlementCost(
        pos.strike, pos.quantity, uDec, cDec
    );
    // Buyer pays → contract → seller (net of keeper fee)
    // Contract sends underlying → buyer
}
```
